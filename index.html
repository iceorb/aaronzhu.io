About

My work centers on data-driven and AI decision making in critical situations. This spans from using unstructured data to create safer communities on petabyte-scale systems TikTok—to detecting health conditions and contraindications based on patient notes and socioeconomic indicators.

My research surrounds the technical foundations of what makes AI safer and more robust, and creating (and testing) safeguards against adversarial attacks and malformed data.

I'm a University of Wisconsin Lorraine A. & W. Dwight Stone Scholarship Recipient for my work on healthcare economics. I'm also a 2x hackathon winner, both times for my use of AI in healthcare. And I'm currently training for a half marathon that I hope to complete after graduation. Alongside English and Mandarin, I can also speak (in varying capacities) French, Korean, and Japanese.
